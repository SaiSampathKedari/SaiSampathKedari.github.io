About Me

I work at the intersection of Robotics and AI, with a strong focus on mathematical and statistical approaches to robot learning and inference. I hold dual master’s degrees from the University of Michigan, where my training emphasized probabilistic modeling, estimation, and optimization.

My current work centers on building and understanding learning and inference algorithms from first principles, with particular attention to uncertainty, identifiability, and failure modes. Rather than relying on off-the-shelf tools, I focus on developing a deep, mechanistic understanding of how algorithms behave and why they succeed or fail.

Prior to graduate school, I worked as a C++ software developer at Dassault Systèmes and Altair Engineering, contributing to large-scale engineering software. This background strongly influences my approach today, with an emphasis on rigor, structure, and implementation clarity.

Research Interests

My primary interest is in robot learning grounded in mathematics and statistical inference, especially in settings where uncertainty and limited information play a central role.

My current strengths and focus areas include:

  • Bayesian Inference and Filtering for Robotics: State, parameter, and belief estimation using Bayesian methods, Monte Carlo sampling, and probabilistic filtering.
  • Statistical Foundations of Learning: Understanding learning algorithms through the lens of estimation theory, uncertainty quantification, and probabilistic modeling.
  • Sampling-Based and Probabilistic Algorithms: Monte Carlo methods, particle methods, and diagnostics for reliability and robustness.

I am also strongly interested in expanding into the following areas, where my mathematical background allows me to ramp up quickly:

  • Learning-Based Control: Revisiting control and optimization-based methods through modern, data-driven and probabilistic perspectives.
  • Reinforcement Learning for Robotics: Developing a principled understanding of RL, particularly where it intersects with model-based methods, uncertainty-aware learning, and control.

My long-term goal is to contribute to robotic systems that learn, reason, and act under uncertainty, with methods that are interpretable, reliable, and grounded in strong mathematical foundations.
